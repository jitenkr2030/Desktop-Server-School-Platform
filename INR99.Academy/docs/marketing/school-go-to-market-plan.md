## 2. Target School Segmentation

### 2.1 Primary Targets (Highest Priority)

#### Category A: Affordable Private Schools (Budget Schools)
**Description:** Private schools charging ₹500-2,000/month tuition fees
- Often located in Tier 2 and Tier 3 cities
- Serve middle-class and lower-middle-class families
- Parents are aspirational and willing to invest in education

**Characteristics:**
- Student strength: 200-1,000 students
- Infrastructure: Basic computer lab or computer room
- Parent demographic: Small business owners, government employees, farmers
- Decision maker: Owner/Proprietor (often the principal too)

**Why They Need INR99:**
- Differentiate from competing schools in the area
- Provide value-added service without additional investment
- Retain parents who might otherwise switch to government schools

**Target Cities:**
- Rajasthan: Jaipur, Jodhpur, Udaipur, Kota
- Uttar Pradesh: Lucknow, Kanpur, Varanasi, Agra
- Bihar: Patna, Gaya, Muzaffarpur
- Madhya Pradesh: Bhopal, Indore, Jabalpur
- Maharashtra: Nagpur, Nashik, Aurangabad
- Karnataka: Mysore, Belgaum, Hubli

#### Category B: Low-Cost International Schools
**Description:** Schools offering English-medium education at affordable rates
- CBSE/ICSE curriculum with English as primary language
- Focus on overall development beyond textbook learning
- Parents seek affordable alternatives to expensive international schools

**Characteristics:**
- Student strength: 300-1,500 students
- Infrastructure: Smart classrooms, basic IT setup
- Parent demographic: Young professionals, entrepreneurs
- Decision maker: Management committee or franchisee

**Why They Need INR99:**
- Enhance curriculum with supplementary digital content
- Provide competitive exam preparation without extra cost
- Offer professional skill development courses

#### Category C: Residential Schools (Budget Segment)
**Description:** Affordable boarding schools in semi-urban and rural areas
- Cater to students from remote areas whose parents work in cities
- Provide accommodation along with education

**Characteristics:**
- Student strength: 100-500 students
- Infrastructure: Limited but functional
- Parent demographic: NRI, Gulf migrants, urban professionals
- Decision maker: Trust/Society managing the school

**Why They Need INR99:**
- Students have limited access to tuition after school hours
- Boarding school environment requires self-study materials
- Parents want visibility into child's learning progress

### 2.2 Secondary Targets

#### Category D: Government Schools (PPP Model)
**Description:** Government schools open to public-private partnership
- May have existing digital infrastructure under various schemes
- Looking for supplementary content to improve learning outcomes

**Approach:**
- Partner with state governments through education departments

**Key States to Target:**
- Bihar, Jharkhand, Odisha, Chhattisgarh (lower HDI states)

#### Category E: School Chains and Groups
**Description:** Organized school chains with multiple branches
- Professional management with centralized decision-making
- Looking for scalable solutions across all branches

**Target Chains:**
- EuroKids, Kidzee (pre-school to primary)
- Podar Education Network
- Springdays School
- Mitr Public School
- Local/regional chains

**Advantages:**
- Single contract covers multiple schools
- Centralized deployment and management
- Higher volume discounts available

#### Category F: NGO-Run Schools
**Description:** Schools operated by educational NGOs
- Focus on underprivileged students
- Limited budget but high impact potential

**Partnership Model:**
- Offer discounted/free access for students
- NGO funds subscription as part of their program
- CSR funding from corporations can subsidize costs
